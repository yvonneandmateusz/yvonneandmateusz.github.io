On the day

The ceremony will be held on September 24th, 2016 at 5:30pm sharp on the hilltop outside the main building of the McMichael art gallery. However, guests are encouraged to arrive early and visit the gallery free of charge. Cocktail and dinner reception will follow with bar service and dancing until late. Shuttles will be provided to our partnered hotel at the end of the night.

Lodging

We have partnered with Hotel Novotel Toronto Vaughan who are providing a discounted rate of $135 CAD per night for both Friday and Saturday nights. The hotel is conveniently located within walking distance of Vaughan Mills, a large outlet shopping mall, and is a 10 minute drive to Canada’s Wonderland. To reserve a room, please contact the hotel by phone (905-660-0212 or toll free at 1-866-630-2680) or by e-mail (novotel.torontovaughan@accor.com) providing your name(s), a credit card number, and referencing Feng & Johnson Wedding. Rooms can accommodate up to four people, though a surcharge of $15 will apply for the third and fourth guest each. The discount rate is available on the 23rd and 24th of September.

Location

The McMichael Canadian Art Collection is conveniently located northwest of Toronto, Canada. The gallery houses an extensive collection of paintings by the Group of Seven, First Nations and Inuit artists. You are welcome to visit the gallery anytime during the day free of charge; simply mention you are attending our wedding.

Getting around

For out of town guests, the best point of arrival is Toronto Pearson International Airport (YYZ). Another airport, Billy Bishop (YTZ), also has a few offers, but most long international flights arrive at YYZ. Getting to city center from the airport is straightforward: there is an (express shuttle)[https://www.upexpress.com/] from YYZ, and YTZ is a 5 minute walk from the city, where local tram services are available. Nevertheless, we highly recommend you rent a car if you are from out of town, as the McMichael art gallery is not easily accessible by public transportation.
